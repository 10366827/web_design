The Local Area of Galway City

Facilities nearby to the B&B include: Eyre Square Shopping Centre, two sandy beaches, spectacular coastal walks, a variety of restaurants within walking distance, friendly traditional Irish music pubs, horse-riding, golfing, fishing, and a variety of water sports . Bikes are also available for hire.

Traditional Irish Music

Music is a huge part of Dublin's History and traditional Irish music has a home right here Galway. A favourite of Christy Moore and the Dubliners amongst its illustrious past, it has been an attraction not only for tourists from all over the world but is also a favourite haunt of locals, who for seven nights a week come to the bars for the real Irish Music Experience.

Restaurants

Galway has a lot of amazing restaurants. From Italian to Indian and everything in between, there is a lot of choices.

If you looking for high quality, delicious pizza then look no further than The Dough Bros. They provide a huge range of pizzas with all kinds of toppings.All of their pizzas are baked in a wood-fired oven.

Eyre Square Shopping Centre

Eyre Square Centre is Galway City liveliest and most attractive meeting place, incorporating over 70 shops including a mix of local Irish retailers as well as the best known brands from the high street including Penneys, New Look, JD Sports, Mothercare, Specsavers, Vera Moda, Jack and Jones, Dunnes Stores, Burger King and many more. As well as our leading mix of fashion and leisure retailers, Eyre Square Shopping Centre has a selection of cafe style eateries offering shoppers a range of choices from tasty nibbles to coffee and snacks.

Beaches

The beach at Salthill is actually several small beaches separated by outcrops. Some of the beaches are sandy and some pebbly. It is located in Galway city and overlooking Galway Bay, a Special Area of Conservation. The beach is lifeguarded from mid-May to the end of September from 11 am to 7 pm. Salthill beach has been a blue flag beach and enormously popular in fine weather during the summer.

Horse Riding

Feeney's Riding stables is a family run Equestrian Centre established over 20 years ago. Their highly regarded equestrian centre is situated in a unique location - 10 minutes drive from Galway city and Salthill, just off the world renowned Wild Atlantic Way, on the west coast of Ireland and yet immersed in rugged Connemara countryside. The riding school in the centre caters for riders of all ages and experience levels. They provides lessons, scenic treks, Summer, Easter, Halloween and Christmas Pony Camps in addition to private classes for individuals and groups. All riders are paired with a suitable horse or pony to match their individual experience level, be it, beginner, novice, intermediate or experienced. They provide helmets and boots for all and a qualified riding instructor is always on site. Safety is a priority at the stables. They are the closest livery yard to Galway city and cater for both horses and ponies with tailored packages available.

Fishing

With so many lakes, sea inlets and rivers, County Galway is an angler’s paradise. Fishing in Galway is a wonderful experience; peaceful surroundings, majestic mountain ranges and stunning seascapes…what more could you ask for? The choice for the keen angler includes lake fishing on Lough Corrib, sea fishing in Galway Bay, salmon fishing at the weir on the River Corrib or fresh water fishing in the many rivers and mountain streams of Connemara.

Galway Bay Fishing offer great packages, and are offer a great service.

Whether you're living on or visiting the west coast of Ireland or just visiting our great country for a holiday, Sea fishing is a great way to relax and an even better way to see the spectacular west coast of Ireland and it's surrounding islands!

Water Sports

Sea kayak through the busy working docks of Galway Harbour, past old fishing boats and stone piers. Savour the sights and sounds of Galway City as you relax in your kayak, amidst the swans and the occasional seal.

A schedule of sailings will operate daily from Galway Harbour, 10am, 12:30pm, 3pm and 5:30pm, 7 days per week (subject to booking levels), maximum 10 passengers per trip, with individual and group rates available. You can try dinghy sailing, powerboating, keelboat sailing or yacht sailing.

Dive Galway on the doorstep of Galway Bay, The River Corrib and the vast Lough Corrib Lakes offer numerous diving opportunities for visitors to Galway. Dive Galway also offer Nightdives, Shoredives, Boatdiving, Cavediving and Driftdiving.
